My Story

I'm a socially conscious Web developer.

Formerly a researcher, I'm dedicating my efforts to using science and software for good.

I unwittingly got my start in doing eco-friendly Web development by integrating YouTube videos into UC Berkeley's amphibian conservation database, AmphibiaWeb.org.

Professionally, I've worked at research centers and as an independent consultant. Although an analyst on paper, I had often found myself launching websites for environmental campaigns (partering with the United Nations to protect oceans, for example) and I went on to do work for the US government in launching a portal for climate change indicators on their site, globalChange.gov.

My claim to fame is that I spearheaded the research of a first-of-its-kind global water quality dataset. It's now being used by the environmental community. The lessons learned were published so that others could help take on the task.


I believe that software, like science, relies on peer sharing networks and makes the most out of our ability to try new things and learn from our mistakes. Good ideas should be open-source. It's critical to making progress for people and the planet.

Why me?

I'm specially equipped to help you think critically about problems and make impacts on consumers and society.

I work in full-stack Web application development. Using object-oriented programming with Ruby, JavaScipt, APIs, and styling, I can help you create communications platforms that fit your needs.
