About me

I'm a socially conscious Web developer who is using science and software for good.

I currently live in San Diego, California.

I got my start in eco-minded Web development by inserting YouTube videos into my research articles for the world's premier amphibian informatics database at UC Berkeley, AmphibiaWeb.org.

Since then, I've gone to grad school and I worked in consulting after doing stints at public policy research centers. Although considered an analyst, I often found myself launching websites for environmental campaigns (partering with the United Nations to save the oceans, for example) and went on to do work for the US government in helping launch a portal for climate change indicators on the site, globalChange.gov.

My claim to fame is that I spearheaded the development of a first-of-its-kind global water quality dataset. It's now being used by the research community and spurring some innovative crowd-sourced ideas. The lessons learned were published in a peer-reviewed journal so that others could help take on the task.


I believe that software, like science, relies on these peer sharing networks and makes the most out of our ability to try new things and learn from our mistakes. It's a critical way to make progress these days for people and the planet.

I studied at UC Berkeley and Yale's School of Environment. By recently taking up Web + software development at Dev Bootcamp, I'm equipped to help you make an even greater impact on consumers and society.
