About me

I'm a socially conscious Web developer.

A former academic researcher, I'm dedicating my efforts to using science and software for good.

I got my start in eco-friendly Web development by inserting YouTube videos into pages for the UC Berkeley's premier amphibian informatics database, AmphibiaWeb.org.

Since then, I've done post-graduate education in natural sciences and have worked in consulting and at public policy research centers. While a research associate, I often found myself launching websites for environmental campaigns (partering with the United Nations to protect oceans, for example) and I went on to do work for the US government in launching a portal for climate change indicators on their site, globalChange.gov.

My claim to fame is that I spearheaded the research of a first-of-its-kind global water quality dataset. It's now being used by the academic community and spurring some innovative ideas. The lessons learned were published so that others could help take on the task.


I believe that software, like science, relies on these peer sharing networks and makes the most out of our ability to try new things and learn from our mistakes. Good ideas should be open-source. It's critical to making progress for both people and the planet.

Why me?

I'm especially equipped to help you make impacts on consumers and society while thinking critically about problems.

I work in object-oriented programming and full-stack development. Using JavaScript, Ruby-on-Rails, APIs, with added-on libraries, databases, and styling, I can help you create a communications platform that fits your needs.
