About me

I'm a socially conscious Web developer.

Formerly an academic researcher, I'm dedicating my efforts to using science and software for good.

I got my start by inserting YouTube videos into pages for UC Berkeley's amphibian informatics database, AmphibiaWeb.org. Now I place high value in doing eco-friendly Web development.

Professionally, I've worked at public policy research centers and as an independent consultant. Although a researcher, I had often found myself launching websites for environmental campaigns (partering with the United Nations to protect oceans, for example) and I went on to do work for the US government in launching a portal for climate change indicators on their site, globalChange.gov.

My claim to fame is that I spearheaded the research of a first-of-its-kind global water quality dataset. It's now being used by the research community. The lessons learned were published so that others could help take on the task.


I believe that software, like science, relies on peer sharing networks and makes the most out of our ability to try new things and learn from our mistakes. Good ideas should be open-source. It's critical to making progress for people and the planet.

Why me?

I'm especially equipped to help you make impacts on consumers and society while thinking critically about problems.

I work in object-oriented programming and full-stack development. Using JavaScript, Ruby-on-Rails, APIs, with add-on libraries and styling, I can help you create communications platforms that fit your needs.
