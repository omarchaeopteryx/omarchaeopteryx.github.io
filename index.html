About me

I'm a socially conscious Web developer using science and software for good.

I currently live in San Diego, California.

I got my start in eco-friendly Web development by inserting YouTube videos into research articles for the world's premier amphibian informatics database, AmphibiaWeb.org.

Since then, I've gone to grad school and have worked in consulting after doing stints at public policy research centers. Although an analyst, I often found myself launching websites for various environmental campaigns (partering with the United Nations to protect oceans, for example) and I went on to do work for the US government in launching a portal for climate change indicators on their site, globalChange.gov.

My claim to fame is that I spearheaded the development of a first-of-its-kind global water quality dataset. It's now being used by the research community and spurring some innovative ideas involving crowd-sourcing. The lessons learned were published in a peer-reviewed journal so that others could help take on the task.


I believe that software, like science, relies on these peer sharing networks and makes the most out of our ability to try new things and learn from our mistakes. Good ideas should be open-source. It's critical to making progress for both people and the planet.

Why me?

I studied at UC Berkeley and Yale's School of Environment. By taking up Web+software development at Dev Bootcamp, I'm best equipped to help you make impacts on consumers and society and think critically about problems.
